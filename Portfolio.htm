College Engineering Projects
Senior Design Capstone Project - Micro Class UAV
  • Designed and fabricated a Small Unmanned Aerial System with a team of five mechanical and aerospace engineering students. This project involved research into prior works, including a review of existing patents for similar UAV systems; a thorough design study and simulation of the UAV; fabrication of a flightworthy prototype of the aircraft; sourcing and procuring necessary materials for the project while maintaining a strict budget; and testing and evaluation of the aircraft performance in comparison to the simulated models.
  • Unfortunately, the aircraft was destroyed during a routine flight test. An NTSB-style investigation of the crash was conducted by the team. Pilot error was determined to be the primary factor in the crash; a major secondary factor was that the design of the aircraft (with a pusher styler propeller) was difficult for an amateur pilot to control.

Left: Solidworks CAD model of the aircraft, used for fabrication purposes and fit checks.
Center: The completed aircraft model on the first day of ground testing.
Right: The aircraft after the crash during test flight.

Airfoil Analysis with XFOIL and XLFR5
  • Conducted analysis of several different airfoil shapes at different Reynold's numbers and angles of attack to determine lift curves, drag coefficients, and stall angles.
Thin Plate Bending Theory and Finite Element Analysis of an Aircraft Wing
  • Created a 3D CAD model of an aircraft wing and conducted FEA on the structure to determine wing loading and deflection while under load.

Left: Planform view of the wing used for Finite Element Analysis.
Right:  Deflection of the wing mode led as a flat plate under load.

Simulink Spring-Mass System
  • Using MATLAB Simulink, a damped spring-mass system was simulated and the response to various dmaping forces were observed and recorded.
Wind Tunnel Experiment and CFD Analysis
  • Conducted CFD analysis on a CAD model of multiple airfoil sections and found the drag coefficient of the CAD model. Then, tested a 3D-printed model of the airfoil in a wind tunnel to validate the drag coefficient calculated from the CFD analysis.
  • Compared the results from the simulation and from the wind tunnel model and analyzed potential sources of error that could have caused any discrepancies betweed the models.
  • Imagery: NACA 0012 Airfoil in a CFD wind tunnel at 15 degree AoA and 10m/s airspeed, and a zoomed in image of the same.
Propeller Stability Investigation
  • Designed and constructed a dynamic model of a two-propeller aircraft to analyze the effects that rotating propellers have on the stability and roll moment acting on the aircraft. The model was suspended from above by spring scales attached to the lifting surfaces of the aircraft body so that the precise in-flight forces could be measured.
  • Also investigated were the effects of different engine-out scenarios on the yaw and roll moment.
  • This project was conducted in a team of four.
Various Aerodynamic, Structural, Electrical, and Signal Experiment Tools

Programming and Analytical Methods
Nonlinear Pendulum Analytical Calculation
  • Analytically calculated the period of a nonlinear pendulum using the 5-Point Gauss-Legendre rule using both MathCAD and MATLAB. The calculation was performed for various levels of damping on the swing pendulum.
Excel VBA Automated Bill of Materials Script
  • While at GAI, automated a system to create the bill of materials for the reinforcing steel in concrete bridge structures, for insertion into plan sets. The script takes user input from an Excel spreadsheet and outputs the table in a format suitable for the plan set, in accordance with FDOT standards. This automation reduced the workload of the structures group by approximately 30% for one project.
Logic Circuit Design

Rate of Cooling  of a Fin
  • Used the Gauss-Seidel method in MathCAD to analytically solve a tri-diagonal system of equations generated from the finite difference model for a triangular profile fin.
